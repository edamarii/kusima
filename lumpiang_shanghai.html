Lumpiang Shanghai

Ulam Recipe

There's something iconic about lumpiang shanghai, but there's also something different about my mother's lumpiang shanghai. It is a taste that is indescribable yet, is something that you constantly look for. I've tasted a lot of different lumpiang shanghai in my life, but nothing has ever compared to hers. Perhaps it is my bias, but I think people who have tasted her cooking can agree on this.

Ingredients

  • The Filling:
  • 1 ½ lbs ground pork
  • 2 pieces of onion, minced
  • 2 pieces of carrots, minced
  • 1 ½ teaspoons of garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ½ cup chopped parsley
  • 1 cup grated cheese
  • ½ cup grated pandesal / white bread
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 ½ tbsp salt

Instructions

  1. Combine all filling ingredients together in a bowl, and mix them well together.
  2. Scoop around 1 spoon of the filling and place on a piece of lumpia wrapper. Spread the filling and fold both sides of the wrapper. Brush beaten egg mixture on the top end of the wrapper. Roll the wrapper until the filling is completely wrapped.
  3. Repeat steps 1 & 2 until you've used up all the filling.
  4. Heat oil in a cooking pot. In medium heat, deep fry the lumpia until it floats.
  5. Remove from the pot with a pair of thongs. Place on a plate with paper towels.
  6. Serve hot, and enjoy!
