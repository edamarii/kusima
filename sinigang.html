Sinigang

Ulam Recipe

No matter the time of the day, or the weather that day, I always felt like sinigang was the type of ulam I can eat no matter what. It was food that my mom frequently cooked, and has become my comfort food to this day. She perfected the sour taste of the broth, the tender pork, and the perfect mix of flavors. Until now, her sinigang is undefeated in my heart.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork sinigang cut
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 large tomatoes, cut into four
  • 1 medium onion, cut into four
  • 2 tbsp patis
  • 6 pieces of gabi, peeled and cut in half
  • 2 siling haba (green)
  • 1 eggplant, sliced to half inch thick half-rounds
  • 4 pieces of okra
  • 1 pack of sinigang mix
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 bunch of kangkong

Instructions

  1. Rinse pork ribs, then drain well.
  2. Over medium heat, put pork ribs and add enough water to cover the pork in a pot. Bring to a boil. When white clumps form, remove them with a spoon.
  3. Once the broth is clear, add tomatoes, onion and patis. Lower heat and let simmer until the meat is tender. Add more water when necessary to maintain the original water level.
  4. Add Gabi, and cook for about 2-3 more minutes.
  5. Add eggplant, okra, and siling haba (Green) cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  6. Add Sinigang Mix to the pot, and stir until the mix is completely dissolved in the water.
  7. Add Kangkong and continue to cook for about 1 more minute.
  8. Serve hot, and enjoy!
