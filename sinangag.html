Sinangag

Agahan Recipe

Iconic fried rice, sinangag isn't anything too fancy but it hits the spot in the early mornings. It's aroma wafting across the dining area, and you take it in with whatever food you choose to pair it with. Bonus points if you drink something warm like coffee or hot choco.

Ingredients

  • 3 Cups cooked white rice (best if kaning lamig or leftover rice)
  • 5 cloves of crushed garlic
  • 2 ½ tbsp of cooking oil
  • salt
  • Optional: 1 eggs

Instructions

  1. Using a wide pan, pour oil and spread across the entire pan.
  2. As the oil heats up, add in the crushed garlic. Don 't add in the garlic when the oil has already heat up, add them during the process instead. This allows the garlic to cook slowly.
  3. Throw in the rice and stir the ingredients slowly. Cook for 3-5 minutes.
  4. Add Gabi, and cook for about 2-3 more minutes.
  5. Add eggplant, okra, and siling haba (Green) cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  6. Optional: If you want to add egg to your garlic rice, pour the egg as you cook and stir the ingredients. Constantly stirring, and allow the egg to break apart.
  7. Serve hot, and enjoy!
