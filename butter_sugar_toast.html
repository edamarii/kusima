Butter Sugar Toast

Merienda Recipe

My siblings and I used to debate how to cook this simple merienda our mom taught us when we were kids. It was an easy way to eat during the afternoon, but it's incredibly addicting. The slight saltiness of butter met with the sweetness of sugar is best paired with a cold juice or any cold and refreshing drink! As a kid though, I preferred having eaten with the butter spread before being toasted. As I grew older, I've learned to appreciate the other way of making this.

Ingredients

  • White Bread
  • Butter (something like Dairy Creme)
  • Sugar

Instructions

  1. Get the amount of slices of white bread that you want.
  2. If you want your toast to be softer with the crusts crunchy, spread butter before toasting. Toasting with the butter will make the white bread soften and soak in the butter, but still toast the crusts or edges of the bread.

    If you want your toast to be toasted and crunchy all the way, spread butter immediately after toasting. Spread while the bread is still hot, or else it won't melt. This allows the butter to still soak into the bread without making it too soft.
  3. Put them in the oven toaster and toast for about 1 minute or until the crusts are golden brown.
  4. Sprinkle as much sugar as desired on top.
  5. Serve hot, and enjoy!
