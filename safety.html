Kitchen Safety

The Basics

Safety in the kitchen should be done mindfully and consciously. There's a lot that can go wrong while you are in the kitchen, so read below to make sure you, and your companions at home are left unharmed.

How to hold a knife

  1. Grip the knife handle with your dominant hand.
  2. Have your forefinger and thumb hold the back of the blade of the knife. This part isn't fully necessary, and you can still chop without having your fingers on the back of the blade. But, this method is more preferred.
  3. Your non-dominant hand will be used to push the ingredient you are chopping. Make sure that it forms a claw.

Oil Safety

If you're anything like me who fears the splatters of oil.

  • » Be careful of hot cooking oil.
  • » Clean oil spills right away
  • » Heat cooking oil slowly
  • » Add food gently and slowly to the pan, do not dunk them in hot oil
  • » When oil begins to smell bad, smoke excessively, and taste burnt. Dispose of it immediately.

Disposing of Used Cooking Oil

  • » Wait for the oil to cool completely.
  • » After the oil has completely cooled, transfer it to a non-recyclable container. Containers may be: milk cartons, wax or plastic-lined containers or take-out containers.

Fire Hazards

  • » Never leave cooking food unattended on the stove, and pay attention to what you are cooking.
  • » Remove flammable, and combustible items like potholders, towels, rags, etc. away from heat sources such as stoves.
  • » Turn pot handles away from the ledge, and out of reach by children. Direct them inward, or away from you. This way, it lessens the risk of being bumped, or grabbed.
  • » Don't wear loose long-sleeved clothing when cooking. Roll sleeves, or wear short sleeved clothing.
  • » Don't use wet oven mitts or potholder as it may pose a risk of scalding when the moisture is heated.

Kitchen Sanitation

  • » Use correct food storage. Make sure leftovers are placed in sealed containers.
  • » Wait for hot/warm food to cool down before placing them in the fridge or freezer.
  • » Don't leave food out for too long (more than 2 hours).
  • » Place frozen groceries immediately in the freezers. This includes chilled groceries.
  • » Avoid cross contamination.
  •     -Don't use the same plate for raw and cooked food.
  •     -Don't place them near each other, or leave them beside each other.
  •     -Don't use the same chopping board for meat, dairy, vegetables, and bread.
  •     -Clean your chopping boards after use.
  • » Wipe down countertops and keep your space clean as you cook. Same goes for the sink.
  • » Consistently wipe down surfaces to avoid left over bits of food fostering bacteria.
